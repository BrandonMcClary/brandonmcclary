Meet The Panther: Jaelan Phillips

Jaelan Phillips’ path to Carolina has already included setbacks, reinvention, and flashes of elite edge talent. Now the Panthers are betting big on what he can become.

By Brandon McClary March 31, 2026 Panthers

Before he was one of the most physically gifted edge rushers in football, Jaelan Phillips’ career almost ended before it really began.

Coming out of high school, Phillips was one of the top recruits in the country and committed to UCLA Bruins football with sky-high expectations. Early in his college career however, things started to go the wrong way. A series of injuries, most notably of which being multiple concussions, forced Phillips into a position no athlete ever wants to be in.

After severe contemplation, he medically retired from football.

It looked like his career was over before it even got started. For a player with that level of talent, it was a brutal turn. Instead of continuing on the field, Phillips stepped away from the game entirely and focused on school and even music for a period of time.

Being the competitor Phillips is, he couldn’t stay off the field for long.

After some time away, Phillips made the decision to return to football, this time transferring to the University of Miami, he was now a Hurricane. This was only the beginning of his rehabilitation.

The comeback at Miami

In 2020, Phillips broke out in a major way. He quickly became one of the most disruptive defensive players in the country, showing elite burst off the edge, strong hands, and the kind of bend that NFL teams look for in their top-tier pass rushers. He finished the season with 8 sacks and 15.5 tackles for loss, re-establishing himself as one of the top prospects in the nation.

That one season was all it took.

Edge Rusher Jaelan Phillips during his days in Miami.

Phillips declared for the NFL Draft and was selected in the first round by the Miami Dolphins. Talent was NEVER the issue for Jaelan Phillips, the lingering concern had always been his ability to stay on the field. His story demonstrates an overwhelming amount of drive and character, some of the intangibles professional teams salivate over.

At the NFL level, Phillips continued to develop into a high-level edge presence. His combination of size, speed, and technique made him a consistent threat off the outside, and when healthy, he showed flashes of being a true difference-maker.

Through four-and-a-half injury riddled seasons on the Miami Dolphins, Phillips racked up 26 sacks, 28 tackles for loss, and 61 quarterback hits.

On November 3rd, 2025, a rebuilding Miami team sent Jaelan to the Philadelphia Eagles for a third-round pick, where he would play the remaining eight games of the season, once again showing how dominant he could be when healthy. Recovering a fumble, and getting to the quarterback twice in just his first game in new threads.

Now, he arrives in Carolina.

A major bet by Carolina

Carolina made Phillips the centerpiece of their free agency class, handing him a massive four-year deal worth $120 million, with $80 million guaranteed. That kind of investment doesn’t come without expectations.

They’re asking him to change things. For a team that lacked heavily in the pass rush department, he’s expected to be a complete difference maker.

Dan Morgan has decided to bet on the flashes he’s shown throughout his career in hopes that he can finally turn into something consistent.
