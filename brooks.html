The Inevitable Return of Jonathan Brooks

Jonathan Brooks has already dealt with more adversity than most young backs ever face, but Carolina still believes his long-term upside is worth the wait.

By Brandon McClary March 30, 2026 Panthers

Jonathan Brooks, a 22-year-old running back out of the University of Texas, was selected 46th overall by the Carolina Panthers in the 2024 NFL Draft. After redshirting his freshman year and seeing limited action the following season, Brooks finally broke out during his sophomore campaign, posting 1,425 scrimmage yards and 11 total touchdowns.

Right as he was establishing himself as one of the top backs in college football, Brooks suffered a torn ACL. While the injury did impact his draft stock, the consensus remained the same. He was still viewed as the best running back in his class.

Pre-Draft Scouting Breakdown

Brooks entered the draft as one of the most complete backs available. His strengths were clear.

He has light, quick feet and elite stop-start ability, allowing him to manipulate defenders in tight spaces. His contact balance stands out, consistently bouncing off tackles and extending plays. As a receiver, Brooks showed reliable hands and a wide catch radius, making him a real threat in the passing game. He also demonstrated strong vision and versatility, fitting both zone and downhill run schemes.

However, there were concerns.

His experience as a true lead back was limited to just one season, leaving questions about long-term durability and consistency. At times, he would try to do too much, passing up simple gains for riskier plays. While explosive, he was not always a true breakaway threat in the open field. And given the strength of Texas’ offense, some evaluators questioned how he would perform in less favorable situations.

Panthers RB Jonathon Brooks (#25)

Still, the upside was undeniable.

The Panthers front office, led by General Manager Dan Morgan, fully understood the risks tied to Brooks’ recovery and drafted him anyway. That decision alone showed how highly they valued his potential.

Brooks made his NFL debut in Week 12 of his rookie season, logging just five snaps and two carries for seven yards against the Kansas City Chiefs. It was a small step, but still a meaningful one.

Unfortunately, the momentum did not last.

In Week 15 against the Philadelphia Eagles, Brooks suffered another torn ACL. It was a brutal setback that reset his recovery process entirely.

Back to square one.

As a result, Brooks missed his entire sophomore NFL season, focusing solely on rehab and getting back to full strength. Despite the setback, the organization remains confident in his future.

In a March 3 interview, head coach Dave Canales stated that the expectation is for Brooks to be ready for the 2026–27 training camp and OTA period.

When he returns, Brooks will enter a competitive backfield featuring veteran Chuba Hubbard, second-year back Trevor Etienne, and free-agent addition AJ Dillon.

But make no mistake, the opportunity will be there.

If Brooks can return to even a fraction of what he showed at Texas, the Panthers may have found their long-term answer at running back. And after everything he has been through, his comeback is not just possible.

It feels inevitable.
